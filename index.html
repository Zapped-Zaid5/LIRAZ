Liraz Nanaz

The Scavenger Brother - Master of Environment

Background, Personality & Motivations

Identity and Background

Basic Information

Liraz Nanaz is one of the two main protagonists in Revival: Lost, alongside his brother Zaril. Both brothers are 60 years old and have been separated since they were 10 due to their intense rivalry.

Humorous Education

While Zaril has a PhD in Psychology, Liraz claims to have a PhD in "floor" as a witty retort during their Uno match competitions.

The Separation

The brothers' rivalry was so intense they were "about to nuke the world to win" when they were just 10 years old. Responsible parents separated them for 50 years. They are eventually reunited by The Diabolic, who uses their current trauma and places money around to bring them back together.

Personality Traits

Liraz is portrayed as the "scavenger brother" who is chill and "vibes" with the new female Genie character. He finds humor in situations, particularly finding Zaril's nickname "nerdillious Wierdo" (given by the Genie) to be hilarious.

He's competitive and uses various taunts and strategies during games, but he also finds competitions boring after extended periods. Despite this, he continues to engage with Zaril's challenges, showing both his competitive spirit and his occasional weariness with their constant rivalry.

Core Motivation

Like Zaril, Liraz's primary motivation is his rivalry with his brother. Their obsession with competing against each other literally keeps them alive in the haunted house. The game explores the theme: "What is the result of obsession?"

If one brother dies, the other also dies because without the competition, they lose their reason to avoid the ghosts. They need competition, not cooperation, to survive - their rivalry is their lifeline.

Gameplay & Abilities

Environmental Combat System

Scavenger Combat Style

Liraz focuses on using the environment and found items to defeat enemies:

  • Yeet cardboard boxes - Throws boxes at enemies like the daughter to stun them
  • Throw dust - Uses dust to suffocate enemies
  • Inventory management - Must strategically manage found items
  • Environmental awareness - Uses surroundings as weapons

Note: Unlike Zaril, Liraz cannot use enemy remains in combat.

Trash Talk System

Verbal Combat Mechanics

Liraz and Zaril use trash talk as a survival mechanic against ghosts:

  • Utter ignorance - Causes panic in enemies
  • Quipping - Creates confusion
  • Insulting - Disrupts ghost behavior
  • Breakdancing - Disorients enemies

Upgradeable Stats:

  • Vocabulary
  • Volume control
  • Delivery Timing

Liraz's trash talk can be combined with dust combos for enhanced effectiveness. Trash talking certain family members can trigger protective frenzies from other ghosts.

Genie Wish System

Liraz can use wishes granted by the Genie to obtain unique items such as "gold cord pizza" or a giant flashlight to blind family members, complementing his environmental combat style.

Notable Competition Moments

Uno Strategy

Attempts to use multiple skip and reverse cards against Zaril but ultimately loses when Zaril uses a "swap decks" card. Liraz forgets to say "Uno" at a crucial moment.

Sleep Contest Victory

After the Uno match, Liraz agrees to a sleep contest with Zaril and wins, loudly celebrating his victory over his brother.

Connect 4 Tactics

Initiates a Connect 4 match and plays strategically, attempting to trap Zaril's pieces and create chains, but ultimately loses to his brother's psychology tactics.

Culinary Challenge

Participates in cook-offs with Zaril, attempting to make Chicken Tikka Masala using detailed recipes during their culinary competitions.

Genie Interactions

Liraz is described as "vibing" with the Genie character, showing a more relaxed and friendly approach compared to Zaril's initially skeptical attitude. He finds entertainment in the Genie's nicknames for his brother.

Game Endings & Lore Role

Liraz can be the protagonist who escapes, cures family members, or interacts with the Mother in various game endings. However, in certain bad endings, if he goes "too deep" in the secret parts of the game, The Diabolic will take him as his vessel to destroy the universe.

In the "Bevrious" sequence, if Liraz and Zaril reach the Sinus section, they are dismembered and die. At the CANON end of the game, both brothers eventually join a multiversal anti-diabolic group.

Media Presence & Voice Acting

Liraz is featured in the game's main theme song, described as a rap battle between him and Zaril. He's also depicted on the potential box art in a sword fight with his brother, with Sinus looking at a family photo behind him.

The game's creator, Zapped Zaid, hopefully casts Jason Griffith to voice Liraz Nanaz, though this is wishful thinking and not yet guaranteed.
